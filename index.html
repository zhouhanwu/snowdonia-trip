Before we begin

Three things worth flagging

  • No train from Llanberis. The village has no mainline station — the only train home leaves from Bangor. Day 4 is routed via Bangor 18:11 Avanti, Euston 21:35.
  • Mountain weather is real in April. Snow and ice on the summit ridge is common. Check the Met Office Mountain Forecast (mwis.org.uk) on Friday night and have a lower-level Plan B ready.
  • Bus times need verifying. The Sherpa'r Wyddfa network runs well but timings shift by season. Check traws.cymru the week before and screenshot the timetables — signal is patchy in the park.

I
Friday 24 April · Travel

London to the foot of the mountain

Afternoon train to Bangor, Sherpa bus through the pass, a slow wander around Llanberis, then a final short hop up to Pen-y-Pass before dark.

  1. 10:30

    Arrive London Euston

    Be at the platforms 10–15 min before departure. Grab breakfast and water for the train.

  2. 11:103h 23m

    🚆 Avanti West Coast · Direct

    London Euston → Bangor (Gwynedd)

    Arrive 14:33 · ~£40–70 advance single · Trainsplit for possible savings

  3. 14:35

    Bangor Station

    Sherpa T2 bus stop is right outside the station. Confirm next departure on the TfW screen.

  4. 14:50~45 min

    🚌 Sherpa'r Wyddfa · T2

    Bangor → Caernarfon → Llanberis

    £3 single · cash or contactless · check exact time before travel

  5. 15:35

    Arrive Llanberis · dump bags

    Small village at the foot of Snowdon. Spar for supplies for tomorrow. If the YHA at Pen-y-Pass can't store bags early, the Electric Mountain café will hold them for an hour or two.

    afternoon
  6. 16:00~90 min

    National Slate Museum

    Free entry. Actually genuinely good — old quarry workshops, the largest working waterwheel in mainland Britain, slate-splitting demos. UNESCO context for the whole landscape around you.

    free UNESCO
  7. 17:30

    Llyn Padarn lakeside · golden hour

    Flat 15-min walk along the lake with Snowdon massif behind you. The iconic lone tree (Tree on a Lake) sits here — even if it's full of Instagram people, the light this time of evening is worth it.

  8. 18:00

    Early dinner · Pete's Eats or The Heights

    Pete's Eats: the legendary climbers' café, cheap hearty plates, pint-mugs of tea. The Heights: proper pub with rooms. Either works. Eat early — bus up to Pen-y-Pass is last service.

    £ hiker food
  9. 19:30~20 min

    🚌 Sherpa'r Wyddfa · S1 · Last bus up

    Llanberis → Nant Peris → Pen-y-Pass

    £3 · VERIFY exact last bus time on day · fallback = taxi from Llanberis (~£20)

  10. 19:50

    Check in · YHA Snowdon Pen-y-Pass

    Stone-built hostel at 360 m, right at the trailhead. You'll walk out of the front door onto the Pyg Track tomorrow morning. Café/bar on site for a nightcap. Lights out early.

    Night 1 of 3 ~£35 pp
  11. 21:00

    Kit prepped · alarms set · sleep

    Lay out tomorrow's clothes. Fill water bottles. Charge headtorches. Set three alarms (two humans will snooze through one). Sunrise is 05:55 and you want to be moving by 05:00.

II
Saturday 25 April · Summit

Sunrise up, lakes down

Pyg Track in the pre-dawn dark, summit for the low-angle light, down past Glaslyn and across the Llyn Llydaw causeway. Afternoon bus to Betws-y-Coed and celebratory pub supper.

⚠ Weather-dependent Check the Met Office Mountain Forecast (mwis.org.uk) on Friday night. If the summit is in cloud with high winds, swap to the Llanberis Path (less exposed, walkable from the village). If it's proper snow/ice on the Pyg zigzags and you don't have microspikes, bail — Cwm Idwal is a stunning Plan B.
  1. 04:30

    Wake up · porridge · check conditions

    YHA kitchen will be quiet at this hour. Quick breakfast, one last look at the forecast, fill hot drinks in flasks. Don't overload the stomach.

  2. 05:00

    Start the Pyg Track · headtorches on

    Path climbs stone steps immediately, heading SW along the ridge above Llyn Llydaw. Headtorch for the first ~40 min before daylight fills in.

    pre-dawn
  3. 05:55

    Sunrise · Bwlch y Moch viewpoint

    The Pyg opens out at Bwlch y Moch ("pass of the pigs") about 50 min in — first big view down into the Llyn Llydaw cwm. Sun comes up over the Glyderau to the east. This is the money shot.

    🌅 sunrise
  4. 06:30

    Climb the Pyg zigzags

    The Pyg and Miners' merge at the base of the zigzags — rocky, steeper, hands-on in places. This is where ice lingers latest in spring. Steady pace, spread out if it's busy.

  5. 07:30

    Summit · Yr Wyddfa · 1 085 m

    Touch the trig point. Photos. Hafod Eryri café may be open (check — April opening varies). The wind at the summit is always bigger than you expect; hood up and find shelter on the lee side to eat.

    🏔 highest point in Wales
  6. 08:15

    Descend to the Miners' junction

    Back down the zigzags to where the paths split. Take the Miners' Track — signed, heads east/NE down towards Glaslyn.

  7. 09:15

    Glaslyn · the deep blue lake

    Trapped in a corrie directly under the summit cliffs. Water is eerily blue-green from dissolved copper. Good spot for a proper sit-down break.

  8. 10:00

    Llyn Llydaw causeway

    The stone causeway across the lake is the iconic Miners' Track image. In low light and still water it's mirror-smooth. From here the path is gently graded all the way back to Pen-y-Pass.

  9. 11:00

    Back at Pen-y-Pass · collect bags

    Rough total: 6 hrs, ~11 km, ~720 m ascent. Collect rucksacks from YHA. Warden centre has toilets and a water refill point.

  10. 12:30~35 min

    🚌 Sherpa'r Wyddfa · eastbound · via Capel Curig

    Pen-y-Pass → Betws-y-Coed

    £3 · confirm service/number at the Warden Centre — routing changes seasonally

  11. 13:15

    Arrive Betws-y-Coed · check in

    Pretty stone village where three rivers meet. Check in at your booking (Vagabond Bunkhouse, a Capel Curig B&B, or an Airbnb in Betws). Hot shower. Legs up.

    Nights 2 & 3
  12. 14:30

    Celebration pub lunch

    Stables Bar at the Royal Oak, or Y Stablau — both do proper post-hike plates. Pint. You've just climbed Wales.

    ££
  13. 19:00

    Dinner · Olif tapas or Hangin' Pizzeria

    Betws has more variety than you'd expect. Olif is small-plates Spanish-Welsh fusion, Hangin' is wood-fired pizza. Book ahead if you can — Saturday nights fill up.

III
Sunday 26 April · Recovery walk

Cwm Idwal · Darwin's glacial lab

A slow morning, gentle 2-hr loop around a perfect glacial lake in the Glyderau, then Betws-y-Coed all afternoon — forest walks, cafés, and time to properly wind down.

  1. 09:00

    Slow breakfast · Alpine Coffee Shop

    Betws's best coffee, good pastries, mountain-people vibe. Eat slowly. Your legs have earned it.

  2. 10:15~25 min

    🚌 Sherpa'r Wyddfa · westbound

    Betws-y-Coed → Capel Curig → Ogwen Cottage

    £3 · ask driver for "Ogwen" — it's a specific bus stop

  3. 10:45~2 hrs

    Cwm Idwal loop · 3.5 km

    A perfect glacial amphitheatre. Path circles the lake with the Idwal Slabs rising on the left and Tryfan's silhouette behind you. Darwin came here in 1831 and realised the valley was carved by ice — there's a plaque. Optional extension up to the "Devil's Kitchen" cleft for the more energetic.

    easy Nat. Nature Reserve
  4. 13:15~25 min

    🚌 Sherpa'r Wyddfa · eastbound

    Ogwen Cottage → Betws-y-Coed

    £3 · return service — check times on the way out

  5. 14:00

    Late lunch in Betws

    Something quick — the Bistro Betws-y-Coed, or grab filled rolls from Cotswold Outdoor's deli. Save appetite for a proper dinner.

  6. 15:00flexible

    Afternoon options in Betws

    Pick based on energy. Low effort: Fairy Glen (short wooded gorge, £1, 45 min). Medium: Swallow Falls via the Afon Llugwy river path (£2 entry, 4 km return). Bigger: Llyn Elsi forest loop (2 hrs, steep-ish up through trees to a reservoir with views back to Betws).

    choose your own
  7. 18:00

    Drink · Stables Bar

    Welsh ales, pub dogs, quiet Sunday evening vibe. Or the Ty Gwyn Inn (17th c. coaching inn, atmospheric) if that's more your speed.

  8. 20:00

    Dinner · pack for tomorrow

    Early night — Monday is a travel + sightseeing day and you'll want to be out by 08:30. Pack and put bags by the door.

IV
Monday 27 April · Coast & home

Castles, coast, the long train home

Conwy Valley line down to the coast, a castle morning, an afternoon in Bangor, then the Avanti direct from Bangor — arriving Euston in time for a late supper.

⚠ About Llanberis Llanberis has no mainline station, so the train home must depart from Bangor. I've routed the day Conwy → Bangor → train. If you wanted Llanberis for the Snowdon Mountain Railway or something else specific, let me know and I'll rework.
  1. 08:30

    Check out · walk to Betws station

    Station is a 5-min walk from most of the village. Grab a coffee and a pastry for the train.

  2. 09:10~35 min

    🚆 Transport for Wales · Conwy Valley Line

    Betws-y-Coed → Llandudno Junction

    Verify exact times (Sunday & Monday services differ) · ~£7 single

  3. 09:55~3 min

    🚆 Change at Llandudno Junction

    → Conwy (one stop)

    Or walk over the estuary bridge in 20 min · saves nothing really

  4. 10:15~2 hrs

    Conwy · castle & walls

    UNESCO World Heritage. Castle itself (£13, ~90 min) has spiral staircases up every tower. Town walls are free and give great rooftop-level views of the harbour. Look for the Smallest House in Great Britain (6ft wide) on the quay.

    £13 castle UNESCO
  5. 12:30

    Lunch · Edwards of Conwy

    Award-winning butcher/bakery on the High St. Dragon scotch eggs, Welsh oggies, cracking sausage rolls. Takeaway onto the quay for a sit-down if the weather's kind.

    Mon open
  6. 14:00~20 min

    🚆 Transport for Wales · coast line

    Conwy → Bangor (direct)

    Frequent service · ~£8 · check platform changes at the Junction

  7. 14:30~3 hrs

    Bangor afternoon

    Pick one: Penrhyn Castle (National Trust, ~30 min walk or quick bus from town — huge neo-Norman pile, genuinely impressive, allow 2 hrs). Or Bangor Pier (Victorian, second-longest in Wales) + Bangor Cathedral + coffee in the university quarter. Castle wins if anyone's a history person.

    Penrhyn £14 NT
  8. 17:30

    Back to Bangor Station

    Allow time for a quick dinner or supplies for the train — the station café is fine but not great. Holyhead Road has better options 5 min walk away.

  9. 18:113h 23m

    🚆 Avanti West Coast · Direct home

    Bangor → London Euston

    Arrive 21:34 · book advance for best fares · seat reservations worth it

  10. 21:35

    Arrive London Euston · home

    Tube home. Sleep for a week.

    ✦ fin

Kit

What to bring

The non-obvious stuff first. April in Snowdonia is genuinely winter-like on the summit ridge — pack for cold and wet even if the valley forecast looks mild.

On the mountain

  • Waterproof jacket (proper shell, not showerproof)
  • Waterproof overtrousers
  • Warm mid-layer (fleece or light down)
  • Hat + gloves (yes, in April)
  • Hiking boots (worn in, waterproof)
  • 2 L water + bladder/bottles
  • Real food: sandwiches, flapjacks, chocolate
  • Headtorch + spare batteries (pre-dawn start)
  • Power bank
  • Suncream + sunglasses (snow glare is real)
  • Blister plasters + basic first aid
  • Microspikes (~£20, split 3 ways, for icy zigzags)

General travel

  • 2 changes of clothes
  • Base layers / pyjamas for hostel
  • Quick-dry towel (YHA often charges)
  • Wash kit (small)
  • Earplugs (hostel dorm / snoring friends)
  • Charger + multi-USB plug
  • Phone in dry bag / waterproof pouch
  • Card + small cash (bus drivers sometimes cash-only)

Viewing the map

The topographic map uses real OpenTopoMap tiles with contour lines, actual trail markings, and true-to-scale positioning of every waypoint and hiking route. The trade-off is that the map tiles need to load from a server on the internet, which some embedded previews block.

Option 1 · Open locally

  • Caveat: on mobile you'll need to transfer the file to your phone first (AirDrop, iCloud Drive, Google Drive, email to yourself).

Option 2 · GitHub Pages (free, permanent URL)

  • 1. Create a free GitHub account if you don't have one.

  • 3. Upload this file, renamed to index.html.
  • 4. Repo Settings → Pages → Source: "Deploy from branch" · main · /root · Save.
  • 5. After ~1 minute you'll have a URL like yourname.github.io/snowdonia-trip. Bookmark on phone.

Option 3 · Netlify Drop (easiest)

  • 1. Go to app.netlify.com/drop.
  • 2. Drag this HTML file onto the dropzone.
